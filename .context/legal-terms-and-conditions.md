## 7. Health, Safety & Physical Requirements

### 7.1 Risk Disclosure (HSWA 2015)
Under the Health and Safety at Work (Adventure Activities) Regulations 2016 and Regulation 8A (effective 1 April 2024), Operators have an explicit duty to inform participants of serious health and safety risks before booking, before the activity, and during the activity. Specific risk information will be provided by the relevant Operator prior to participation.

Adventure tourism activities involve inherent risk including adverse weather, difficult terrain, altitude, physical exertion, wildlife, and equipment factors.

### 7.2 Health and Fitness Requirements
Each activity has specific minimum health, fitness, and physical capability requirements set by the Operator. Clients are responsible for confirming all participants meet these requirements prior to booking.

Pre-existing medical conditions, physical limitations, or allergies must be disclosed at time of booking.

### 7.3 Weight and Physical Dimension Requirements
Aviation operations impose strict CAA-mandated weight limits. If a participant exceeds the stated limit at the time of the activity, the Operator may decline participation without refund. Accurate weights must be declared at booking.

### 7.4 Liability Waivers
Operators may require participants to sign a risk disclosure form or liability waiver prior to the activity. Under Section 28 of the Health and Safety at Work Act 2015, no waiver can exclude or limit an Operator's obligations under that Act. Waivers serve as evidence of informed consent only.

### 7.5 Accident Compensation (ACC)
New Zealand's ACC scheme provides no-fault personal injury cover for physical injuries sustained in NZ. ACC cover may affect any right to sue for personal injury under NZ law. This does not remove any Operator's duty of care.

### 7.6 Participant Conduct
Participants must follow all safety instructions from the Operator, pilots, guides, and crew. The Operator retains the right to remove a participant who poses a safety risk, with no refund applicable.

### 7.7 Travel Insurance
Traverse South strongly recommends comprehensive travel insurance covering: trip cancellation, emergency medical evacuation, medical expenses, loss of personal effects, and personal liability. Traverse South is not responsible for losses that could have been covered by adequate travel insurance.

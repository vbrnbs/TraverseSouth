# Traverse South: Social Media Strategy (Instagram & LinkedIn)

This document establishes the tactical strategy, visual direction, posting schedule, and content-sourcing blueprint for Traverse South’s social media presence.

---

## 📸 1. Instagram: "The Wilderness Archive"
**Objective:** Sell the vibe, the raw adventure, and the scale of the Southern Alps. Think of Instagram as a premium digital photo album that *amazes* people. It should look like an editorial photography journal, not a cheap travel agency page.

### 🎨 Visual & Aesthetic Rules
*   **The Grid:** High-contrast, moody, dark, and visceral. Avoid oversaturated tourist photos. Prefer deep greens, granite grays, glacial blues, and shadows.
*   **Minimal Branding:** Do not overlay cheap logos, graphics, or text on the photos/Reels. The image must stand alone.
*   **No Placeholders:** If using graphics (e.g., text carousels), use the Traverse South brand guidelines (strict `#0b0b0b` background and rare, tactical `#f36458` Coral-Red highlights).

### 🗂️ Content Pillars
1.  **Visceral Scale (The Vibe):** Low-frequency audio, slow-pan Reels of helicopters hovering near massive granite walls, packrafts in deep glacial gorges, and remote saunas surrounded by snow.
2.  **Vetted Operators (The Guarantee):** Behind-the-scenes profiles of our legendary local operators (e.g., Jeremy at Packraft Queenstown). Show them in their element — repairing gear, studying river charts, or flying helis in heavy weather. This builds immediate trust.
3.  **Expedition Details:** Carousel posts showing maps of a multi-day route, followed by raw photos of the terrain at each stop.

### 📅 Posting Schedule & Formats
*   **Frequency:** Crawl-walk-run approach. Start with **1 post per week** to protect your time while working full-time. Scale to **2 posts per week** after a few weeks, and only increase further once a steady routine is established.
*   **Content Options (Alternate when posting 1x/week, post both when posting 2x/week):**
    *   **Option A (Reel - Vibe):** A 7-10 second atmospheric clip of NZ wilderness. Sound: Ambient wind, river currents, or helicopter blades.
    *   **Option B (Carousel - Experience):** 4-5 high-resolution slides. Slide 1: An amazing hero shot. Slide 2: A simple topographic map of the route. Slides 3-4: Close-ups of technical gear or a wood-fired sauna. Slide 5: Minimal CTA text.
*   **Instagram Stories:** 1-3 times a week. Real-time updates, client testimonials, or quick behind-the-scenes raw footage. Keep it low-overhead and spontaneous.

## 💼 2. LinkedIn: "The High-Performance Reset"
**Objective:** Target high-earning, time-poor professionals, founders, and VC/PE partners. Reframe extreme adventure not as a distraction from work, but as the *ultimate tool* for executive mental resets, clarity, and leadership alignment.

### ✍️ Tone & Style Rules
*   **The Voice:** Authoritative, uncompromising, and zero-bullshit.
*   **The Angle:** The juxtaposition of intense corporate stress vs. the absolute clarity of wilderness survival. 
*   **The Core Hook:** Discomfort in the wild makes you a sharper decision-maker in the boardroom.

### 🗂️ Content Pillars
1.  **The "Switch Off" Protocol (Disconnecting to Reconnect):** Discussing how a phone-free, off-grid expedition in the Southern Alps, followed by a sauna + ice bath, acts as a hard reboot for a fatigued C-suite executive.
2.  **Wilderness vs. Boardroom (Executive Psychology):** Drawing parallels between running a rapid in a packraft or navigating a glacier and making high-stakes business pivots under uncertainty.
3.  **The Zero-Admin Thesis (Solving Logistics):** Highlighting the pain of planning and the chaos of NZ weather cancellations. Sell the B2B benefit of our Zero-Admin Protocol and Seamless Pivot (if weather hits, we handle everything; the executive team keeps moving).
4.  **Survival-Grade Team Trust (High-Performing Teams):** Redefining corporate team-building. In the wilderness, trust is not a soft skill or a boardroom slide—it is a physical dependency. When navigating a freezing alpine canyon or running rapids, you rely on the split-second decisions of the person next to you. In these environments, team trust can literally save lives. Bringing this level of absolute, life-saving accountability back to the boardroom builds unbreakable team cohesion.

### 📅 Posting Schedule & Formats
*   **Frequency:** Start with **1 post per week** (e.g., every Tuesday or Thursday) to fit around your full-time job. Scale to **2 posts per week** once a routine is established.
*   **Content Options (Alternate when posting 1x/week, post both when posting 2x/week):**
    *   **Option A (Text + High-End Image):** A personal thought leadership post from the founder’s perspective. Hook focuses on stress, decision fatigue, or the myth of traditional "spa retreats."
    *   **Option B (PDF Document Carousel or Text):** A tactical post detailing why high-growth companies are booking B2B Wilderness Offsites, explaining survival-grade trust, or detailing our weather pivot protocols.

## 🔓 3. Sourcing Premium Content (For Free)
As a new brand, you do not need to spend $10,000 on custom photography shoots. You can source world-class, premium assets for free using the following channels:

### Channel 1: Our Vetted Operators (The Goldmine)
*   **Why:** Operators like Jeremy (Packraft Queenstown), local helicopter charters, and luxury lodges have accumulated *gigabytes* of raw, authentic, high-resolution media over decades of running expeditions. They rarely have the time or marketing team to edit or post them.
*   **The Pitch:** 
    > *"Hey Jeremy, we're building out the Traverse South launch campaign. If you have any raw footage/photos of packrafting on the Dart River or backcountry camps, send them over. We'll edit them, run them on our platforms, tag/credit Packraft Queenstown, and drive direct booking leads back to your Rezdy backend."*
*   **Action:** Create a shared Google Drive or Dropbox folder and send the link to your operators. 

### Channel 2: Tourism New Zealand Visuals Library
*   **Why:** Tourism New Zealand maintains an official, high-resolution database of photos and videos designed specifically to promote NZ tourism. It is **100% free** for travel businesses to use.
*   **Where:** Visit [Visuals Library - Tourism New Zealand](https://visuals.tourismnewzealand.com/).
*   **What to Search:** Focus on "Southern Alps", "Fiordland", "Helicopter", "Alpine Tarn", "Milford Sound", "Mount Aspiring", and "Sauna". Filter for high-resolution images and raw vertical video clips.

### Channel 3: Curated Free Stock Platforms (Moody Aesthetic)
Standard stock photos look cheap and corporate. To maintain a "Quiet Luxury" look, use Unsplash and Pexels with specific search rules:
*   **Search Queries:** *"Moody mountains New Zealand"*, *"glacier aerial hike"*, *"wood fired sauna forest"*, *"ice bath cold plunge cabin"*, *"foggy river canyon"*
*   **Curation Rule:** If it looks like a happy family on a tourist trail, discard it. If it looks like an isolated adventurer facing a massive, moody peak, save it.

### Channel 4: UGC & Creator Collaborations
*   **Why:** Micro-influencers and adventure videographers frequently travel through Queenstown and Wanaka. They have incredible content but lack distribution or monetisation.
*   **The Deal:** Partner with them. In exchange for booking them onto one of our vetted single-day activities (which we bundle at cost), they grant Traverse South perpetual usage rights for all content captured during the trip.

## 🛠️ 5. Social Media Workflow (The "Lean Founder" Protocol)
To ensure social media doesn't consume your time:
1.  **Batch Creation (Sundays - 1 Hour):** Collect 3 Reels (footage from operators/Tourism NZ) and write the captions in a simple Google Doc.
2.  **Scheduling Tool (Buffer / Later):** Spend 15 minutes scheduling the posts for the week.
3.  **Engagement (15 mins/day):** Only log into LinkedIn and Instagram to reply to comments, engage with 5 target operator accounts, and connect with 5 potential B2B executive prospects. Keep the apps off your phone during working hours.
